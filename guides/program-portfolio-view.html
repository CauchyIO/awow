Only the program can clear these

4 open

The whole point of the tier. These aren’t bugs or stories — no team can fix them from inside its own board. They’re allocation decisions: re-sequence, re-staff, or cut scope. Each names the move it unlocks.

A1
Platform is at 132% — six initiatives funnel through it
Every high-priority initiative needs Platform. It’s the program’s true constraint; the ranking is meaningless until its load is under 100%.
moveDefer I7 (DR & resilience) to the next increment → Platform drops to ~108%; then re-time I5 to land under capacity.
A2
I3 blocked on Security sign-off — Security is committed to I1
Two of the top three initiatives (I1, I3) compete for the same Security capacity in the same window. A team can’t resolve a cross-initiative priority clash.
moveSequence I1’s sign-off ahead of I3, or pull in external Security review for I3.
A3
Architecture is single-threaded across I1 and I5 design reviews
One reviewer is the bottleneck for two initiatives’ design gates. This is a staffing decision, not something the Architecture team can absorb.
moveAdd a second reviewer for the increment, or stagger the two design gates two weeks apart.
A4
I4 can’t fully size until I2 delivers lineage
A forecast gap, not a block: I4’s sizing depends on I2. Fine as sequenced — flag it so the dependency isn’t mistaken for a stalled team.
watchHold I4’s commit until I2 hits 60%; revisit at the next plenary.
This is a lens, not a board. There’s no second source of truth up here. Initiatives route down to the team boards as stories; sizes and progress roll up from them. The program owns priority and allocation; the teams own sizing and delivery. They meet at the plenary — where teams see the ranked, allocated portfolio and commit. Run this view on teams that aren’t on awow boards and it has nothing to roll up.
