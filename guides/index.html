Why awow exists

Above a certain size, every organisation develops a fog: the question what is actually happening right now? gets three or four answers depending on who you ask. awow clears it by making the board the single source of truth and giving an agent the context to keep it honest. Every flow above earns its place by how it touches that one board.

Daily · loops

The delivery loopthe everyday rhythm

If the board is where the surgery happens, this loop is the surgery. Through the day /refinement-prep and /process-workitem run again and again — drafting a feature, taking a story to PR — and /daily-checkin caps the day by reconciling it onto the board. It’s the loop one person can run alone, and it’s what proves the value.

The thinking behind it

Where these guides come from

The guides are the hands-on layer. The reasoning underneath — the coordination fog, the board-as-graph reframe, ceremonies rethought, the prompt landscape, governance and works-council considerations, and a worked rollout — lives in the published Agentic Way of Working methodology and its Boskalis case study. Each flow above distils the idea you need to use the guides; the full argument is the long read.

Flows, not steps. There’s no sequence to march through and no badge for finishing. Most teams lean on the delivery loop first and pick up the others when a real need shows up — each runs on its own clock, and they only ever meet at the board.

Source of truth for every command and skill referenced here: .agents/commands/ and .agents/skills/. This index is self-contained — no external fonts, links, or scripts; links point only to the sibling guide files in this folder.
