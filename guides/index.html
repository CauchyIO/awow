Three scopes, three centers of gravity

Almost everything in this repo lives at the team tier, where the board is the single source of truth. Two tiers bracket it: an individual below, working at the session, and a pillar above, working at the service map across teams. Zooming out doesn’t just widen the view — the thing at the center changes. That is why the pillar tier feels like a different kind of place: it is.

New to awow?

Start at the team tier. Run /setup-awow to wire the board, then take one real story through the delivery loop — /refinement-prep, then /process-workitem to a PR. That single turn of the loop is what proves the value; the individual and pillar tiers wait until you want them.

Why awow exists

Above a certain size, every organisation develops a fog: the question what is actually happening right now? gets three or four answers depending on who you ask. awow clears it by making the board the single source of truth and giving an agent the context to keep it honest — then lets that same discipline scale down to the individual and up to the pillar.

Daily · loops

The delivery loopthe everyday rhythm

If the board is where the surgery happens, this loop is the surgery. Through the day /refinement-prep and /process-workitem run again and again — drafting a feature, taking a story to PR — and /daily-checkin caps the day by reconciling it onto the board. It’s the loop one person can run alone, and it’s what proves the value.

03

Pillar · cross-teamcenter of gravity · the service RACI

One tier above the team. A pillar doesn’t have a board — it has the service map that says which team owns what, and it governs the boundaries between several teams’ boards at once. It is a different archetype: it publishes contracts down to teams and aggregates their signal up, where a team repo only ever consumes.

▲

This leaves the team’s board behind. If the rest of this page felt like one team’s world, that’s because it is. Here the center of gravity becomes the activity×team RACI, the rhythm is event-triggered rather than daily, and the work is governing boundaries — not shipping stories. Proposed, not yet built.

The thinking behind it

Where these guides come from

The guides are the hands-on layer. The reasoning underneath — the coordination fog, the board-as-graph reframe, ceremonies rethought, the prompt landscape, governance and works-council considerations, and a worked rollout — lives in the published Agentic Way of Working methodology and its case study. Each flow above distils the idea you need to use the guides; the full argument is the long read.

Scopes, not steps. There’s no sequence to march through and no badge for finishing. Most teams live at the team tier and lean on the delivery loop first; the individual tier sharpens the people doing the work, and the pillar tier appears only once several teams need their boundaries governed together. Each runs on its own clock.
