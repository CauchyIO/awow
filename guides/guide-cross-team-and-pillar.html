Two feedback loops + a plan↔execution bridge

Three mechanisms across the team↔pillar seam — and why a pillar is a different archetype, not a bigger team.

A team loop that runs on a clock, a pillar loop that runs on evidence, and a bridge that keeps the plan and the work in sync. Companion to proposals/cross-team-feedback-and-service-drift.md.

draft · for review pillar tier 3 mechanisms

TL;DR

Feedback on the awow stays inside a team and runs on a weekly clock. Re-defining a service is a different kind of thing: it lives between teams and up at the pillar, and must run on accumulated evidence, not a cadence. Keeping the plan and the board reconciled is a third thing again — a continuous sync. A pillar is therefore a different archetype (an internal node, not a leaf), and the object it turns on is one activity×team RACI: services distil up from it, project routing decomposes down through it.

The problems this must solve

P1 · steer resources

No live picture of who's working on what, so leadership can't see where to add or shift people.

P2 · plan ≠ execution

The planning model (goals, services, dependencies) and the board (epics/stories) don't reflect each other.

P3 · services undefined

Routing and classification are ambiguous; the definition drifts as ambiguous work arrives.

P4 · run-the-business as projects

Planned work, maintenance, and incidents are managed as one undifferentiated stream.

P5 · invisible blocks

Cross-team critical-path blocks surface only after they've already sunk a deliverable.

P6 · adoption risk

While everything is novel, people must both feel and be heard.

Coverage. P3 + P6 → the two loops + the RACI spine.  P1 + P2 + P5 → the plan↔execution bridge.  P4 falls out of P2 + P3.

The principle both loops share

People feel heard when their input produces a visible change — or a recorded, reasoned no. People are heard when the system captures their reality whether or not they had words for it in a meeting.

Stated feedback

What someone says in a retro. The voiced channel.

Revealed feedback

What their sessions actually show — usage-coach, digests. The channel that survives a thin week.

Closure for both loops is the same mechanic: the change lands as a git diff. The repo is the podium — "heard" becomes auditable instead of vibes.

Two loops, two triggers

Loop 1 · cadence-triggered

Feedback ON the awow

Inside one team's repo. Fits awow today.

  • Trigger: the clock — a weekly 20–30 min retro, kept frequent while the learning rate is highest.
  • Rotate the convener; the substrate (usage-coach) drives, the human just convenes.
  • Always close with a diff to conventions or a recorded "heard X, chose not to act because Y."
Loop 2 · event-triggered

Service-definition drift

Between teams + up to the pillar. Ascends out of team scope.

  • Trigger: evidence, not a clock — reviewing a definition on a cadence destabilises it.
  • Log, don't meet: every time work won't fit the RACI, one line in the exceptions log.
  • Threshold: revise when N same-failures pile up, or one severe collision/gap appears.

The tier tree — leaf vs internal nodes

"Team vs pillar" is really leaf vs internal node in a tree, and it nests (team → pillar → department → org). Same substrate shape everywhere; only the role differs. The sharpest difference is direction of data flow.

internal node (pillar) leaf node (team) contracts published down signal aggregated up

A leaf only consumes; a pillar is bidirectional — publishes down + aggregates up. That bidirectionality is the new machinery.

One activity RACI is the spine

The primitive is the activity — the lowest-granularity unit of work, concrete enough to assign unambiguously (which is why it beats fuzzy "services"). One RACI maps activities → teams. Services are distilled up from it; project routing decomposes down through it. There is no separate "service RACI" — service drift is activity-RACI drift.

The invariant — exactly one Accountable team per activity

ActivityServiceAccountable
Ingest raw eventsData ingestionTeam A
Normalise schemaData ingestionTeam C
Reconcile shared recordsShared records⚠ Team A & B
The ways the invariant breaks are the drift triggers.
  • Gap — an activity with no Accountable team → coverage hole.
  • Overlap — an activity with ≥2 Accountable teams → boundary conflict.
  • Service drift — a service whose activities scatter across teams incoherently.

All visible at activity granularity. Each unplaceable activity becomes one line in the exceptions log.

How the event cadence runs

01LogWork won't fit the RACI → one line in the exceptions log. No meeting.
02AccumulateThe log gathers evidence over time. The steward watches it.
03Threshold tripsN same-failures, or one severe collision / gap.
04Revision PRA team authors a change to the RACI. Reviewed in the open.
05Ratify & publishPillar ratifies; merge publishes the new pinned version down.
Guardrail. Teams author their boundaries (the PR); the pillar ratifies coverage coherence — ratification, not dictation. The moment the tier-up hands teams their definitions, the autonomy that drives adoption dies and "feel heard" dies with it.

The plan↔execution bridge

The two loops and the RACI govern the definition of work — what each service is, which team owns it. They do not keep the plan and the execution in sync. That join — the seam — is where P1, P2 and P5 live, and it decides whether a portfolio roll-up is truth or fiction. It's a sync, not a feedback ritual.

Loop 3 · continuous sync

Two directions, both continuous

  • Down — translation. A plan node decomposes into activities, each routed to its Accountable team by the RACI and sized, via a human-set directive. An activity that won't route is exactly an exceptions-log entry.
  • Up — reconciliation. As work moves on the board, status flows back to the plan node so the portfolio stays current. The leaf agent's existing board-maintenance, extended.

First-class — and not day zero

Maintained by hand, the plan↔board link rots within a sprint — the same drift awow exists to remove, reintroduced one tier up. So the mapping is a versioned, owned artifact, not a one-off integration script.

Sequencing: don't switch it on across the board at day zero — routing is wrong while definitions are fuzzy. Pilot on one well-defined plan node, let the threshold mature the definitions underneath it, then widen.

Source of truth. When the plan already lives in an external EA system, awow doesn't duplicate it — the external system stays authoritative; awow owns only the linkage + up-reconciliation, and even the RACI becomes a pinned projection. Owner: the leaf agent maintains the links; the steward owns the translation directive — no new human role.

Why it's so different

A team repo only ever consumes. A pillar repo publishes contracts down and aggregates signal up — and it runs on accumulated evidence, not a clock. Treating it as "a team repo with extra files" would smuggle in an entire work-pipeline that has no meaning at this tier.

The fix isn't a second product. It's one substrate, parameterised by node role (role: leaf | internal) with a parent pointer — exactly how phase: frontmatter already gates command discovery today.
